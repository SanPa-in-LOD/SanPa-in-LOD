Metadata Alignment

Different metadata standards employ different terms to express the same meaning This is mostly true for metadata standards that belong to specific domain, such as those related to the cultural heritage, because they tend to use more specific expressions in order to describe a particular kind of objects. In this section we align the metadata standards that were used for describing our items in order to normalize the use of different expressions. We have divided the properties that needed alignment in 4 key groups: properties related to the people (Who) inolved, properties related to the time (When) in which the item was created or published, properties related to places (Where) in which the item could be collocated and properties related to the actual content of the items (What). There are cases in which there is no expression available for addressing a specific property of the item. Our suggestion is to state those missing expressions instead of stretching the sense of a particular term to fit the description. We believe that the missing terms represent the discrepancies between the use of a particular metadata standard and, as a consequence, the differences between different cultural domains.
