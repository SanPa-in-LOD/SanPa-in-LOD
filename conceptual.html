Conceptual model

For what concerns the conceptual model the choice fell on using multiple vocabularies and ontologies to optimally describe and represent the heterogeneity of our entities and the "capillarity" of our domain. To keep it simple we organized the items according to three macro-areas: libraries, archives and museums (LAM). We used, RDA and Bibframe for the items belonging to the library domain; OAD, EAC-CPF and RIC-O for archival resources and finally CIDOC-CRM for museological objects. In addition, we used EBUCORE to address some specific or technical tasks, RDF/RDFS for expressing concepts and FOAF for describing authorities. NMM (Nepomuk Multimedia Ontology), was instead used to describe multimedia objects (music, movies, TV series). Furthermore, ELI (European Legislation Identifier) paired with SKOS and XML-Schema were used to describe a specific entity pertaining the legal field. Ultimately, CIDOC-CRM, FRBRoo and DublinCore were used for harmonization purposes and to give to our conceptual model a solid yet simple structure.

For the graphical rendering we used Professor Silvio Peroni’s extension palette “Graffo” in yED in order to have a simple diagram that enabled an immediate visual distinction between classes and properties.
